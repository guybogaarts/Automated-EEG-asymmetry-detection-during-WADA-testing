Another limitation of our approach lies in the fact that the annotations provided by the clinical neurophysiologist are used as the golden standard. Our results show that the clinical neurophysiologist  focusses, whether consciously or not, on the central and frontal brain regions. However, because of the geography of the vasculature in the brain, intra-
carotid amobarbital would likely affect the central areas of the brain, such as the motor strip, more than it would the
mesial structures such as the hippocampus ﻿(Bouwer 1993). In fact, The duration of slow waves in the mesial temporal region are significantly shorter than that in the central region ﻿(Bouwer 1993).
