EEG acquisition

Ag/AgCl EEG electrodes were placed according to the international 10-20 electrode placement system. This electrode placement yields 8 bilateral electrode pairs and 3 midline electrodes. EEGs were recorded at a sampling rate of 256 per second using EEG amplifiers (Schwarzer, type EEG 29 US) and reviewed online with Brainlab software (OSG).

Calculation of the Brain Symmetry Index

EEGs were processed off-line with in-house developed Matlab software (2013). The recordings were band-pass filtered between 0.5 and 25 Hz and divided in 10 second epochs. Subsequent calculation of QEEG measures was performed on uni-polar EEG channels. The BSI is defined as the absolute value of the average relative differences in spectral density beween the left and right hemispheres.
