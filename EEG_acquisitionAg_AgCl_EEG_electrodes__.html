EEG acquisition

Ag/AgCl EEG electrodes were placed according to the international 10-20 electrode placement system. EEGs were recorded at a sampling rate of 256 per second using Schwarzer EEG29US  hardware and reviewed online with Brainlab software (OSG).

Calculation of the Brain Symmetry Index

EEGs were processed with in-house developed Matlab software (2012). The recordings are band-pass filtered between 0.5 and 25 Hz and subsequently divided in 10 second epochs on which subsequently analysis is performed. The BSI is defined as the absolute value of the average relative differences in spectral density of the left and right hemispheres. Our implementation differs slightly from the those published previously (Putten 2004)(Putten 2007)(Putten 2004). This is because it was more convenient to uses Matlab software previously written for EEG based seizure detection (Bogaarts 2014). The spatial BSI (sBSI) is calculated according to equation 1:

\(BSI=\frac{1}{11\cdot8}\Sigma\Sigma\frac{L-R}{L+R}\) equation 1

where e indicates the electrode pair and f  indicates the frequency bin.
First, the spectral density of eleven 2Hz bins ranging from 0 to 16 Hz (1 Hz overlap) is calculated for each EEG channel. Second, the absolute left minus right difference for each electrode pair at each 2 Hz bin are divided by their sum. Third, the average of these values over the 8 electrode pairs is calculated. Finally, the BSI is calculated as the average value over the 11 frequency bins. In addition to changes in spatial symmetry, diffuse change in contralateral EEG characteristics may arise as well due to cross-filling or by a transient functional disconnection from the ipsilateral hemisphere (Tu 2015) (Hong 2000)(Gotman 1992)(Douw 2009)(Ahern 1995).
