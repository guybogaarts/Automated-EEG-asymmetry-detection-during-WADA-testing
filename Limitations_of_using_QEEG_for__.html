Limitations of using QEEG for asymmetry detection in Wada tests

Essential for accurate detection of asymmetry using QEEG is high quality EEG recording. Ways to minimize artifacts include using better fixatives such as collodion or better electrodes such as subdermal wires and minimizing patient anxiety and agitation with a pre-Wada procedure. In this study we used a Kalman filter to smooth the BSI and rFBE time series. In this way, short term artefacts affecting the QEEG measures could be removed. Additional smoothing is achieved by taking the average of BSI and rFBE since both measures are based on different EEG electrodes.
Several mathematical methods may be useful remove artefacts in the EEG before BSI and rFBE are calculated. Such artefact removal methods could be based on independent component analysis (Vos 2011)(Jung 2000), regression based myogenic correction (McMenamin 2009), wavelet approaches (Zima 2012) or Adaptive Filtering methods (Boudet 2008).
