Essential for accurate detection of asymmetry using QEEG is high quality EEG recording. Ways to minimize artifacts include using better fixatives such as collodion or better electrodes such as subdermal wires and minimizing patient anxiety and agitation with a pre-Wada procedure.
Several mathematical methods may be useful remove artefacts in the EEG before BSI and rFBE are calculated. Such artefact removal methods could be based on independent component analysis (Vos 2011)(Jung 2000), regression based myogenic correction (McMenamin 2009), wavelet approaches (Zima 2012) or Adaptive Filtering methods (Boudet 2008). In this study we used a Kalman filter to smooth the BSI and rFBE time series. In this way, short term artefacts affecting the QEEG measures could be removed. Furthermore, the difference in performance with and without Kalman filtering is of similar magnitude as found in EEG based seizure detection (Bogaarts 2014).
Additional smoothing is achieved when cBSI is calculated because BSI and rFBE are based on different EEG electrodes. By taking the average of BSI and rFBE, artefacts present in either electrode pair F3-F4 or C3-C4 are attenuated.
