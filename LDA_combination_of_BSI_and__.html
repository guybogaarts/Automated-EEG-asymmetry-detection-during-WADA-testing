LDA combination of BSI and rFBE 

Based on the results shown in figure 2, BSI calculated using electrode pair F3-F4, and rFBE based on electrode C3-C4 were combined using LDA into the final cBSI score. Figure 5 shows a scatterplot of all rFBE-BSI points, red points mark epochs labelled as ‘symmetric’ and green points epochs labelled as ‘asymmetric’. The orientation of the green points versus to the red points indicate that a (linear) combination of rFBE and BSI better separate ‘symmetric’ from ‘asymmetric’ epochs compared to either rFBE or BSI alone. In each LOO evaluation, a slightly different set of weights was found, (BSI: 0.74+-  and rFBE 0.74 +- ). Because the weights for rFBE and BSI were proximately the same in each LOO evaluation, both weights were set to 0.5 which equates to taking the average of rFBE and BSI. Equation 2 can thus be rewritten as:

cBSI=BSI+rFBE               Equation 5

The blue line in figure 5 indicates the direction perpendicular to the linear combination of rFBE and BSI. Depending on the desired sensitivity and specificity, a line perpendicular to the line in figure 5 can be used for classification. Furthermore, by varying this line, the ROC curve in Figure 6 is constructed.
